About Our Organization

The International Airlines Technical Pool represents "a convention of airlines made up of over 140 Member Airlines, 50 'Sponsored' Airlines, and over 45 OEM and service providers." The organization brings together aviation industry members to discuss resource sharing and cost reduction while boosting operational efficiency. Members collaborate on pooling aircraft recovery kits, spare parts, equipment, line maintenance, and engines for both narrow and wide-body aircraft.

The membership encompasses more than 500 global delegates from a diverse, multicultural community. The organization operates without boundaries, encouraging members to contribute ideas freely and without political bias. IATP maintains a 76-year history promoting safety and has strengthened international airline bonds since its 1948 founding through a gentlemen's agreement model.

The IATP Mission Statement

The organization's mission focuses on airlines sharing technical resources "to generate economic savings and support on time dispatch reliability and operational safety." IATP functions as a non-profit, independent, non-political global organization built on democratic principles with equal opportunities for all member airlines and their representatives.

History of IATP

IATP originated in 1948 when European airlines began sharing aircraft materials, equipment, and manpower resources through reciprocal agreement without financial settlement. The organization expanded significantly by 1950, introducing financial settlement formulas as membership grew internationally.

Key Milestones

1948
European airlines began sharing aircraft materials, equipment, and manpower resources through reciprocal agreement without financial settlement
1950
Introduction of financial settlement formulas as membership grew internationally
1960
Formulation of the "Consolidated Pool"
1967
Name changed to "International Airlines Technical Pool"
1977
Merger with the North American Airlines Technical Pool
2005
Establishment of permanent Luxembourg office
2019
Corporate office relocation
2020
Virtual conference during COVID-19 pandemic
2023
Implementation of new two-tier corporate governance structure
2024
Reached 133 airline members and 43 associate members

Recent developments include the establishment of a permanent Luxembourg office in 2005, corporate office relocation in 2019, the first virtual conference during COVID-19 in 2020, and the implementation of new two-tier corporate governance in 2023, with membership reaching 133 airline members and 43 associate members by 2024.
