About IATP

A convention of Airlines sharing Technical Resources to generate economic savings and support on time dispatch reliability and operational safety.

Our History

The International Airlines Technical Pool (IATP) was established in 1948 with two simple purposes: share the cost of component spares and share the cost of technical resources.

Originally based in Copenhagen, the airline pool moved to Geneva in 1963, where it remained until 1995. Today, IATP is headquartered at the International Air Transport Association (IATA) offices in Montreal, Canada.

Over the decades, IATP has evolved from a small consortium of European airlines to a global network serving 133 member airlines and 43 associate members worldwide. Our commitment to collaboration and resource sharing has remained constant throughout our history.

The organization has continuously adapted to the changing needs of the aviation industry, expanding from simple parts pooling to comprehensive technical support across line maintenance, aircraft recovery, and equipment pooling.

Our Organization

IATP operates as a global aviation consortium where competing airlines cooperate in the technical domain. Our organization facilitates resource sharing across four primary pooling categories:

Spare Parts Pooling

Over 4,000 available items with 14-day free loan periods plus 7 days logistics

Line Maintenance

81 participants across 890 stations worldwide

Aircraft Recovery

12+ recovery kits capable of handling aircraft up to A380 size

Equipment Pooling

248 participants managing 167 pool items

With over 500 delegates worldwide and presence in 890 pooling stations globally, IATP maintains partnerships with more than 40 OEM and service providers to ensure comprehensive support for all member airlines.

Governance

IATP operates under a two-tier governance model implemented in 2023, ensuring effective oversight and efficient daily operations:

Advisory Committee

Provides strategic oversight and guidance to ensure IATP continues to meet the evolving needs of the global aviation industry

Management Board

Handles daily operations and ensures efficient coordination across all pooling activities and member services
