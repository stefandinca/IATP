IATP EVENTS

Events

The IATP Semi-annual Conference offers a multitude of events that allow attendees to connect with over 130 airlines from around the world. This unique venue gives you the opportunity to conduct your pooling business – whether it's spare parts or line maintenance – in the most efficient manner while allowing you to share your industry knowledge, network, reunite with colleagues, and much more!

UPCOMING EVENTS

133rd IATP Conference

St. Julian's, Malta
7 - 11 March 2026

Conference Information

The 133rd IATP Conference will be held in Malta on 7 – 11 March 2026!

IATP Team is working on all the arrangements as well as the membership growth and hopes to welcome new potential members and observers at the Conference.

APU (Auxiliary Power Unit) Project Group

Following the encouraging feedback received during the meeting held at the Bali conference regarding the potential launch of APU as a new IATP pool group, a dedicated project group is now actively developing the initiative. We encourage all members interested in providing or using APUs to contact the IATP Office for more information and to get involved.

Engines Pool Group

We take the opportunity to remind you about the Engines Pool group, aimed to help the IATP member Airlines to resolve quickly, efficiently and with competitive fees unscheduled engine removals. More information about this initiative will be given during the Engines Pool Group meeting at the conference.

AOG Speed Meetings

As usual, two AOG speed meetings sessions will be organized during the conference:

  • Airline-to-Airline
  • Airline-to-Associate Member

The speed meetings will enable the delegates to meet other counterparts, exchange contact details, and briefly check the support opportunities between the companies with the aim to solve AOG situations, when no pooling could be set up in advance.

Introduction Sessions for Newcomers

First-time attendees and all delegates who would like to refresh their knowledge on IATP-related activities will have the chance to attend our Introduction Sessions:

  • Introduction to Parts Pooling
  • Introduction to Aircraft Recovery
  • Introduction to IATP Invoicing
  • Senior Delegate Familiarization Session

New Platform Presentation

The re-engineering of the IATP Platform, executed by Crystal Airlines Solutions and overseen by the office team, continues to progress. Crystal remains on track to present the platform at the Malta Conference and to move it into production once the Summer Season 2026 begins. During the 133rd IATP Conference, two dedicated sessions "New Platform Presentation" will be organized to introduce the new platform. We look forward to showcasing this important milestone in Malta.

Important Reminders

  • Registration is open until January 30 – don't miss the deadline!
  • Guest and Pionair payments must be done online during registration.
  • Please ensure you apply for a visa in advance, if required.

We look forward to meeting you in St. Julian's, Malta!

Malta Travel Information

Visa Requirements

Source: Government of Malta

Non-EU citizens (also known as third-country nationals) may visit Malta by means of a Schengen visa, which allows free movement within the whole Schengen Zone to European Union Schengen members as well as EFTA Schengen members. A Schengen visa is valid for a maximum of 90 days within every six-month period starting from the date of entry.

Please refer to consularplus.gov.mt to receive additional information regarding requirements for your nationality/passport.

If a VISA application is required, third-country nationals are obliged to apply for a visa at the following EU (European Union) Diplomatic Missions and Consular posts, which issue visas in representation of Malta, where Malta has no Diplomatic Mission or Consular post. This website could provide additional information: identita.gov.mt.

If you need an invitation letter for VISA application, you will find the instructions in the Message Center after logging in to the IATP Platform. For non-platform users, the information is displayed on the bottom of the Registration Form.

Electricity

Malta's power sockets deliver an average voltage of 230 v /- 10%. The frequency of the supply is 50 hertz.

It's recommended to bring an adaptor to use your electrical appliances.

Malta uses the type G plug: it has three rectangular pins in a triangular pattern.

Time

Malta uses Central European Standard Time (CET), which is 1 hour ahead of Greenwich Mean Time (GMT +1).

Currency

Malta's currency is the Euro (€/EUR).

The Euro coin series comprises eight different denominations: 1, 2, 5, 10, 20 and 50 cent, €1 and €2. The Euro coins have a common side and a national side. The national side indicates the issuing country
